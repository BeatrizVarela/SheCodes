Momo

The sweet peachy Syrian Hamster




Momo is an adorable Syrian Hamster, who is 1 year and 6 months old. She loves to take sand baths, dig her cage floor and cheese.

She was rescued in Pedroso, Vila Nova de Gaia, and currently lives is Lisbon. She has also travelled to Algarve last year, which makes her a super tourist rodent.

She has a boyfriend - Pompom - who she likes to bite and fight with (and would love to kill him too). They had 7 babies back in March, and 2 of them live with her: Hoshi and Tama.


Syrian Hamsters (or Golden Hamsters) are originate from Syria and were first described and officially named in 1839, by George Robert Waterhouse, a British zoologist.

Waterhouse's original hamster was a female named Cricetus Auratus, meaning "golden hamster". The skin of the animal is kept at the Natural History Museum in London. (like, ew, c'mon!)
